> **At a glance** - Creating a competition involves setting up details, adding participants, leaderboards, and tasks.

Only users with the **editor** role can create competitions. If you have the necessary privilege, you can access the **Competitions** link under the **Editor** section of your left menu. Follow the steps below to create a new competition.

## When to use this
Use this feature when you want to engage users through competitions. Consider creating a competition when:
- You want to motivate participants with gamification.
- You need to track performance through leaderboards.
- You want to gather feedback via tasks like quizzes or surveys.
- You wish to create a collaborative environment among teams.

## Steps

### 1. Navigate to Competitions
Go to **EDITOR > GAMIFICATION AND ENGAGEMENT > Competitions > Add Competition**.

### 2. Add Competition Details
Enter a catchy name and description for your competition. Set the start and end dates. Optionally, you can add a theme and upload a related PDF.

Once you are done entering these details, click on the **Save** button.

### 3. Add Participants
Select the participants for the competition. You can filter participants by Business Unit, Country, Group, Name, Email, and Meta Tags. After selecting the desired participants, click on the **Save** button to save your selection.

### 4. Add Leaderboards
To add leaderboards, click on the **Add** button. In the pop-up, you can choose the type of leaderboard: By Team or By Participants.

- **Visible To**: Choose who can see the leaderboard (Everyone, None, or Only Members in Leaderboard).
- **Scoring Method**: Select either sum or average for scoring.
- **Leaderboard Name**: Provide a name for your leaderboard.
- **Show Time**: Check this option if you want to time the completion status of tasks.
- **Show Ranking**: Specify how many ranks to display.
- **Scoring Duration**: Choose whether scores are added Always or within a specified Date range.
- **Teams**: Select the teams for a team leaderboard or participants for an individual leaderboard.

Once you have entered all required information, click on the **Save** button to create the leaderboard.

### 5. Add Tasks to the Competition
The final step is to add tasks to your competition. You can include various tasks such as Quiz, Survey, Video Coaching, Scorecard, and Challenge. Refer to the respective articles for guidance on how to add these tasks:

## Tips
- Use a clear and engaging description to attract participants.
- Regularly update the leaderboard to maintain engagement.
- Consider the scoring method that best fits your competition's goals.

## Things to know
:::caution
Only users with the **editor** role and the appropriate privileges can create competitions.
:::
